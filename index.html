The Web
is Broken

When you use a web app today, you usually connect to its developers' servers. This is backwards, and leaves them in control.

Sandstorm
puts you in
Control

Sandstorm makes it easy to run web apps on your own server. Bring their apps to your data, and make sure they don't do anything you don't like.

More than
just a VM

No command lines, no configuration files. Apps are installed through an app-store-like web interface. And, most importantly, every app runs in a separate secure sandbox, so an evil app cannot take down your server.

Why have your own server?

It's all about control...

All Your Data in One Place

Why should your personal data be scattered around the internet, stored on computers belonging to dozens of companies? It's your data, not theirs. Keep it all in one place.

No Disappearing Apps

Apps running on your server do not disappear when the developer stops supporting them.

No Spying / Data Mining

Sandstorm apps can only talk to the outside world with your explicit permission. That means they can't secretly spy on you, develop an advertising profile from your data, or perform psych experiments on you without your consent.

End Walled Gardens

Your apps shouldn't only integrate with other apps from the same developer. They should integrate with whatever apps you choose to use. Sandstorm makes it easy.

Stay Secure

Some developers are bad at security. With apps running in a secure sandbox on a server you control, you can defend yourself against poorly-written apps — and even malicious ones.

But what's in it for the developers?

As it turns out, lots of stuff...

Zero Operational Costs

You don't have to pay for servers nor carry a pager. Just make your app work, put it on the app store, and relax.

Just One Machine

Since every end user is running their own instance of your app, you never need to worry about scaling. You can pretend your app only runs on a single machine.

Security is Built-in

You don't have to implement login. You don't even have to implement sharing — Sandstorm does that for you. You can even export APIs and integrate with other apps without thinking about OAuth.

Any Tech Stack

Sandstorm is a native-code sandbox. It supports any Linux-based technology stack. Use whatever language you prefer. Easily port existing code.

Level Playing Field

When users have control, they are more willing to use apps from developers they don't already know and trust. This means new, small developers can break into the market more easily.

Use Our Servers...

We offer managed Sandstorm instances. Starting in March 2014, this service is in closed alpha testing. As we ramp up, we will send invites to people on the mailing list on a first-come-first-serve basis.

Github »

...Or Run Your Own

Sandstorm is Open Source Software. You can find the code and installation instructions at Github. You will need a Linux machine and an understanding of Linux system administration to set up your server, but you can then share that server with friends and family who do not know Linux.

Develop Apps

We need apps! We have a few already, but we need more. Check out the Github project for instructions. Join the developer group to discuss your plans and get help.
